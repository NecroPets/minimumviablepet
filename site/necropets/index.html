          Gently asked

          The questions before the door opens.

          Is this disrespectful — will it even feel real?

          It's meant the opposite way — as reverence. We don't fabricate a stranger or claim to replace your friend. NecroPets listens to their stories, their habits, their sounds, and reflects them back. Many people tell us it feels less like software and more like being remembered by someone they loved.

          Where does my data go?

          Nowhere. NecroPets runs locally, on your own device. Your photos, videos, vet records, voice clips and stories never touch a cloud, never train someone else's model, never leave your machine. Your grief is not a dataset. It stays sovereign, with you.

          What do I need to upload?

          Whatever you have. Photos and videos teach us their face and movement; voice clips teach us their sound; vet and health records ground who they were; and your written or spoken stories carry the soul — the inside jokes, the routines, the small particular love. More is richer, but it's never required.

          What if I have very little of them left?

          Then we begin with what remains, tenderly. A single photo and a few memories are enough to start. NecroPets is built to hold the gaps with care rather than invent — and you can keep adding as more surfaces: a forgotten video, an old voicemail, a story you weren't ready to tell yet.

          Can I talk to them with my own voice?

          Yes. Speak out loud and they listen; they can answer back in a voice rebuilt from your clips of them. Or keep it quiet and type — late at night, when the house is still. However you reach for them, they're there.
