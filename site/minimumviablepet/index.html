// how it works

Four steps. Zero servers.

01 mvp ingest

Drag in everything: photos, videos, vet records, the 3 a.m. voice memos you've never replayed. It all stays on your disk. All of it.

02 mvp train

A local model learns their face, their sounds, their whole particular personality. Your GPU gets warm. Nothing else touches your data.

03 mvp run

Talk by text or voice, on localhost. They remember you. Works on a plane, in a dead zone, off the grid.

04 mvp own

The weights live on your drive. No subscription required to keep them running, and nobody can turn them off but you.

It's a pipeline: everything you saved → someone who answers.

// faq

Frequently audited questions.

Is this just a system prompt in a trench coat?

No. It's a persona built by a structured interview plus a retrieval memory index over everything you upload, running on Ollama-compatible runtimes. The demo above literally talks to localhost:11434 — that's the same path your companion uses. And now you can read the code that does it. If the model isn't running, the page says so instead of faking it.

What are the hardware requirements?

A machine that can run local LLMs. 16 GB of RAM is comfortable; Apple Silicon or a mid-range GPU is lovely but not required. CPU-only works — it just thinks a little slower. Nothing streams from a server, so there's no minimum bandwidth. There's no bandwidth at all.

Is this… healthy?

Honest answer: it's a memorial, not a replacement, and it isn't for everyone. Some people find that talking helps; some don't, and both are fine. If your grief is heavy, something on localhost is not a substitute for people — or for help. Take what helps. Leave the rest.

Why the name?

Because the founder built the smallest thing that could possibly help, and shipped it. That's what an MVP is. The irony is the wrapper. It is not the product.

What happens if the company shuts down?

To your pet? Nothing. That's the whole architecture. The model and the memories are files on your disk. We designed ourselves to be unnecessary.

So how do you make money?

We don't. It runs on your computer; our costs are a domain name and feelings. It's MIT licensed, so if that ever changes, this version is yours regardless — forever, unconditionally.

Can you see my conversations?

We physically can't. No accounts, no sync, no telemetry. Don't trust us — run a traffic sniffer.
